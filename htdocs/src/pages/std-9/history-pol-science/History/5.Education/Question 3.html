Question 3

Explain the following statements with reasons.

Q

1) The District Primary Education Programme was undertaken.

ANS
i) Primary education refers to the schooling given to children in the 6 to 14 year age-group. ii) In 1988, the central government started a scheme for the spread of primary education and to improve educational standards. It is known as Operation Blackboard. iii) District Primary Education Programme was started in 1994 to universalise primary education. It was implemented in seven states including Maharashtra with a vision of 100% attendance, reducing school drop-out rates, education of girls and physically handicapped. iv) It included programmes such as research on and evaluation of primary education, alternative education, creating societal awareness, etc. v) The ‘mid-day meal’ scheme was started in 1995 to provide proper nourishment for students.
Q

2) The NCERT was established.

ANS
i) The National Council of Educational Research and Training was created in Delhi on September 1, 1961. ii) Its primary goal is to assist the Central Government in problems of school education within the framework of a complete policy and in the implementation of educational schemes. iii) The NCERT was in charge of educational research and development, training, extension, educational activities, and the reorganisation of school curricula and textbooks. iv) It has played an important role in developing school curricula and textbooks in collaboration with the Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE). It advises and cooperates with state governments on elementary and secondary education issues. v) It has designed teacher workbooks and handbooks, provided teacher training, developed teaching-learning methodologies, and conducted national talent searches.
Q

3) The farmers were benefited by the Indian Agriculture Reasearch Institute.

ANS
i) The Indian Agricultural Research Institute was given the status of a University in 1958 and work began in departments such as development of the agriculture sector, research, well-equipped laboratories, soil science, agricultural sciences, economic botany and other departments. ii) Research also began on wheat, pulses, oilseeds, vegetables and many other problems. iii) Its most significant achievement is the fundamental research it has conducted on the methods of taking multiple crops in a year, which has been of great benefit to farmers.
